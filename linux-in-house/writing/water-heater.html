Water Heater

Published: 2025-10-08 10:20
Author: Don Cohoon

Tankless Water Heater

I moved into a house with a gas Tankless Water Heater bolted outside the garage wall. In the south it is not suppose to freeze, and indeed, last winter it did not. Most of the piping is covered with foam insulation that is zip-tied onto the pipes.

Like most places the water contains a lot of minerals like limestone, that can slowly clog small openings like the radiator of a quick-fire heater such as this.

I found it simple, after I did it, and hard to put it all together the first time. Hopefully writing it down helps me remember it, and maybe someone else could use some motivation to do this useful annual chore. Replacing a clogged heater is expensive, so I consider it a money saving opportunity.

Supplies

  1. 5 gallon bucket
  2. Two washing machine fill hoses
  3. Submersible pump with hose fitting
  4. 2 gallons of vinegar

Instructions

My Experience

Turn off the thermostat and pull the power to the heater first! Mine was an outside gray metal box with a plastic handle inside you simply pull out to disconnect the circuit.

Turn off the gas to the heater next! Look for a ball/cock valve in-line to the gray metal pipe with no insulation.

Turn off the water to the house next! Usually buried in the ground under some kind of cover, probably green. Mine was on the other side of the house ;-)

  • After the pressure is relieved, use some pliers to unscrew the cold-water filter, clean, and do not over-tighten when replacing. It was very small, but sturdy.

  • When done, pour the old vinegar onto some stubborn weed patch if you have that. It tends to kill most plants.

  • Oddly, the metal hose connection resting in the vinegar for an hour will turn from shiny new, to dull/black old.

Don’t forget to turn the gas, electric and thermostat back on when done!

Reference

1/6HP Portable Utility Pump Submersible

4 ft. Rubber Fill Hoses (2-Pack)

Regards

Hope this helps,
– Don Cohoon
