Bottom Up

Published: 2025-11-01 10:20
Author: Don Cohoon

Top to bottom, or bottom to top; which way to read.

Early in my career computer screens painted their picture on CRT’s from the top left, to bottom right. Like we all learned in school, reading books, newspapers and magazines.

One day new computers became available and switched that upside-down. A blank terminal/screen might start at the top left, but after two dozen commands you will find yourself scrolling from the bottom up and entering commands down below.

But I was used to scrolling from the top down! And my commands were typed on the home position, top left of course, with stuff falling off at the bottom of the screen.

Alas, it took a few hours and I forgot all about it, until I saw a screenshot of my old terminal after 30 years. It inspired me to write an ODT (Operator Display Terminal) interface and enable elapsed time accounting (completed entries) for some of my daily jobs. The top line is always open for input, so I know where to type, no scrolling prompt.
