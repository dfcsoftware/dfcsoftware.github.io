Author: Don Cohoon

Leave Me Alone

That was the most often used phrase on me growing up. No one wanted to know what was inside my head.

Some of the reasons I think about why others do not listen to me:

  1. My voice is too soft/low/mumbling
  2. My stories are not interesting
  3. I don’t demand attention
  4. Does not pertain to them
  5. I give up
  6. I was taught at an early age to be quiet

How to fix that:

  1. Practice clear direct speaking (belly voice)
  2. Engage people with openers that pertain to them (know your audience - Dale Carnegie[1])
  3. Study how to tell stories (Mark Twain[2], Garrison Keillor[3])
  4. Start small to build on success (motivation)

Simply offer friendship to strangers – Garrison Keillor

Reference:

  1. Dale Carnegie: https://www.dalecarnegie.com/en
  2. Mark Twain - How to tell a story: https://www.gutenberg.org/files/3250/3250-h/3250-h.html
  3. Garrison Keillor - How to tell stories: https://www.sfgate.com/performance/article/Garrison-Keillor-tells-how-to-tell-stories-5914678.php

Regards,

– Don Cohoon
